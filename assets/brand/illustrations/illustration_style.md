# ApplyforUs Illustration Style Guide

## Overview
The ApplyforUs illustration style is modern, approachable, and professional. Our illustrations help users understand features, celebrate achievements, and navigate their job search journey with confidence.

---

## Visual Style

### Core Aesthetic
- **Style**: Flat design with subtle gradients
- **Complexity**: Simple to moderate detail
- **Personality**: Optimistic, professional, encouraging
- **Approach**: Human-centered, relatable, aspirational

### Design Language
1. **Geometric Simplicity**: Clean shapes, minimal complexity
2. **Soft Gradients**: Depth without heavy shadows
3. **Rounded Corners**: Friendly, approachable (4-8px radius)
4. **Balanced Composition**: Clear focal points, harmonious layout
5. **Meaningful Details**: Every element serves a purpose

## Gradient Usage

### Brand Gradients

#### Primary Gradient (Indigo to Violet)
```
Linear gradient: 135° (diagonal)
Start: #6366F1 (Indigo-500)
End: #8B5CF6 (Violet-500)
```
**Use**: Hero elements, primary CTAs, key objects

#### Success Gradient (Emerald to Teal)
```
Linear gradient: 135°
Start: #10B981 (Emerald-500)
End: #14B8A6 (Teal-500)
```
**Use**: Success illustrations, achievements, positive outcomes

#### Warm Gradient (Amber to Orange)
```
Linear gradient: 135°
Start: #F59E0B (Amber-500)
End: #F97316 (Orange-500)
```
**Use**: Highlights, energy, call-outs (use sparingly)

### Application Guidelines
- **Gradient Opacity**: 100% for main elements, 20-40% for backgrounds
- **Gradient Angle**: Consistent 135° for brand cohesion
- **Flat + Gradient Balance**: 70% flat colors, 30% gradients
- **Avoid**: Excessive gradients that feel dated or overwhelming

## Character Design

### Design Principles

#### Body Proportions
- **Style**: Slightly stylized, not cartoonish
- **Head-to-Body Ratio**: 1:5 to 1:6 (realistic proportions)
- **Limb Style**: Simplified but natural
- **Hands**: Simplified (3-4 fingers acceptable for small scale)

#### Facial Features
- **Eyes**: Simple dots or minimal detail
- **Expression**: Optimistic, focused, determined
- **Avoid**: Overly detailed faces at small sizes
- **Emotion**: Convey through posture and body language

### Character Diversity

#### Representation Requirements
✓ **Gender**: Balance male, female, non-binary representation
✓ **Ethnicity**: Diverse skin tones, hair types, features
✓ **Age**: Range from early career (20s) to experienced (50s+)
✓ **Body Types**: Varied builds and sizes
✓ **Ability**: Include assistive devices when appropriate
✓ **Attire**: Professional, business casual, range of styles

#### Character Archetypes

**1. The Job Seeker**
- Professional but approachable attire
- Laptop, phone, or resume in hand
- Expressions: Hopeful, determined, focused
- Settings: Home office, coffee shop, library

**2. The Professional**
- Business attire (suit, blazer, professional dress)
- Confident posture
- Settings: Office, conference room, presentation

**3. The Career Changer**
- Transitional, thoughtful expression
- Mix of old/new career symbols
- Settings: Crossroads, doorway, new environment

**4. The Success Story**
- Celebratory gesture (arms raised, fist pump)
- Achievement symbols (trophy, checkmark, confetti)
- Expressions: Joy, accomplishment, relief

**5. The Team Member**
- Collaborative posture
- Group settings
- Expressions: Engaged, supportive

### Clothing & Style
- **Colors**: Navy, gray, white, and brand accent colors
- **Style**: Contemporary professional
- **Avoid**: Dated fashion, overly trendy items
- **Inclusive**: Various cultural attire where appropriate

## Scene Composition

### Setting Types

#### 1. Home Office / Remote Work
**Elements**:
- Laptop on desk (MacBook or generic)
- Desk lamp (simple design)
- Plant (succulent or small potted plant)
- Coffee mug
- Window with simple view
- Minimal clutter

**Mood**: Focused, productive, comfortable

#### 2. Professional Office
**Elements**:
- Desk with monitor
- Office chair
- Bookshelf or filing cabinet
- Whiteboard or presentation screen
- Natural light from window
- Clean, organized

**Mood**: Professional, accomplished, established

#### 3. Interview Setting
**Elements**:
- Two chairs facing each other
- Table or desk between
- Resume/documents on table
- Professional environment
- Good lighting

**Mood**: Important, preparatory, confident

#### 4. Success Moments
**Elements**:
- Achievement symbols (trophy, star, checkmark)
- Confetti or celebratory elements
- Upward arrows or growth charts
- Bright, optimistic colors
- Character in celebratory pose

**Mood**: Triumphant, joyful, accomplished

#### 5. Collaboration / Networking
**Elements**:
- Multiple characters interacting
- Connected nodes or network visualization
- Shared workspace
- Devices showing communication
- Handshake or connection metaphor

**Mood**: Connected, supportive, community

#### 6. Abstract / Conceptual
**Elements**:
- Geometric shapes
- Data visualizations
- AI/tech metaphors (circuits, nodes)
- Minimal or no characters
- Emphasis on concept over realism

**Mood**: Innovative, technological, forward-thinking

### Composition Guidelines

#### Rule of Thirds
- Place focal points at intersection points
- Balance elements across grid
- Avoid centering everything

#### Visual Hierarchy
1. **Primary Focus**: Largest, most saturated, centered
2. **Secondary Elements**: Supporting details, medium size
3. **Background**: Minimal detail, muted colors

#### Depth & Layering
- **Foreground**: Main character/object, 100% opacity
- **Midground**: Supporting elements, 100% opacity
- **Background**: Environment, 60-80% opacity

#### Negative Space
- Allow breathing room (minimum 15% of canvas)
- Don't overcrowd
- Use space to direct attention

## Illustration Categories

### 1. Hero Illustrations
**Purpose**: Main page headers, landing pages

**Specifications**:
- Size: 800×600px to 1200×800px
- Complexity: Medium-high detail
- Characters: 1-3 people
- Scene: Complete environment
- Style: Most polished and refined

**Use Cases**:
- Homepage hero
- Feature page headers
- Marketing materials
- Pitch decks

### 2. Feature Illustrations
**Purpose**: Explain specific features

**Specifications**:
- Size: 400×400px to 600×600px
- Complexity: Medium detail
- Characters: 0-2 people
- Scene: Focused on feature concept
- Style: Clear, conceptual

**Use Cases**:
- Feature cards
- Product tours
- Help documentation
- Onboarding flows

### 3. Empty States
**Purpose**: Communicate absence of content positively

**Specifications**:
- Size: 300×300px to 400×400px
- Complexity: Low-medium detail
- Characters: 1 person or object only
- Scene: Minimal background
- Style: Encouraging, not discouraging

**Use Cases**:
- No search results
- Empty lists/tables
- New user states
- Zero data states

### 4. Success States
**Purpose**: Celebrate user achievements

**Specifications**:
- Size: 300×300px to 500×500px
- Complexity: Medium detail
- Characters: 1 person in celebratory pose
- Scene: Achievement symbols, confetti
- Style: Joyful, energetic

**Use Cases**:
- Application submitted
- Resume completed
- Goal achieved
- Milestone reached

### 5. Error States
**Purpose**: Communicate errors empathetically

**Specifications**:
- Size: 300×300px to 400×400px
- Complexity: Low-medium detail
- Characters: 1 person looking concerned but not distressed
- Scene: Simple, clear problem indication
- Style: Empathetic, helpful (not punishing)

**Use Cases**:
- 404 pages
- Error messages
- Failed uploads
- Connection issues

### 6. Onboarding Illustrations
**Purpose**: Guide new users through setup

**Specifications**:
- Size: 400×400px (square) or 400×600px (portrait)
- Complexity: Medium detail
- Characters: 1-2 people showing action
- Scene: Step-by-step clarity
- Style: Instructional but friendly

**Use Cases**:
- Welcome screens
- Tutorial steps
- Feature introductions
- Setup wizards

### 7. Icon Illustrations
**Purpose**: Small, simple concept communication

**Specifications**:
- Size: 64×64px to 128×128px
- Complexity: Low detail
- Characters: Rarely (object-focused)
- Scene: Single object or simple concept
- Style: Icon-like, simplified

**Use Cases**:
- Feature badges
- Achievement icons
- Status indicators
- Quick reference

## Animation Guidelines

### When to Animate
✓ Loading states
✓ Success confirmations
✓ Onboarding transitions
✓ Feature explanations
✓ Micro-interactions

### Animation Principles

#### Duration
- **Quick actions**: 200-300ms
- **State changes**: 400-600ms
- **Scene transitions**: 800-1200ms
- **Onboarding loops**: 2-4 seconds

#### Easing
- **Ease-out**: Default for most (decelerating)
- **Ease-in-out**: Smooth transitions
- **Spring**: Playful interactions (use sparingly)

#### Properties to Animate
✓ Position (translate)
✓ Scale (grow/shrink)
✓ Opacity (fade)
✓ Rotation (subtle, < 15°)
✗ Avoid: Heavy morphing, complex path animations

### Animation Types

#### 1. Loading Animations
**Style**: Subtle, non-distracting loop
**Duration**: 1.5-2s infinite loop
**Elements**: Dots pulsing, simple rotation, fade in/out

#### 2. Success Animations
**Style**: Celebratory, one-shot
**Duration**: 800-1200ms
**Elements**: Checkmark drawing, confetti burst, character jump

#### 3. Transition Animations
**Style**: Smooth scene-to-scene
**Duration**: 600-800ms
**Elements**: Fade + slide, morph between states

#### 4. Micro-interactions
**Style**: Quick, responsive feedback
**Duration**: 200-400ms
**Elements**: Button press, hover effect, sparkle appear

### Animation File Formats
- **Lottie (JSON)**: Primary format for web/app
- **GIF**: Fallback for email, simple loops
- **MP4**: High-quality, presentations
- **CSS**: Simple transitions and transforms

## Technical Specifications

### File Formats

#### Source Files
- **Figma**: Primary design tool
- **Adobe Illustrator**: Vector refinement
- **Layer organization**: Named, grouped logically

#### Export Formats
- **SVG**: Web, scalable UI illustrations
- **PNG**: Social media, presentations
  - @1x: Standard resolution
  - @2x: Retina displays
  - @3x: High-resolution needs
- **JSON (Lottie)**: Animated illustrations
- **PDF**: Print materials

### Export Settings

#### Web Export (SVG/PNG)
```
Format: SVG (preferred) or PNG @2x
Size: Artboard size (no scaling)
Background: Transparent
Color Profile: sRGB
Optimization: SVGO or similar
```

#### Print Export
```
Format: PDF or high-res PNG
Resolution: 300 DPI minimum
Color Profile: CMYK (print) or sRGB (digital print)
Bleed: 0.125" if applicable
```

### Naming Convention
```
illustration-[category]-[name]-[variant].[ext]
```

**Examples**:
- `illustration-hero-job-search-light.svg`
- `illustration-feature-ai-resume-builder.png`
- `illustration-empty-no-jobs-found.svg`
- `illustration-success-application-sent-animated.json`

## Accessibility Considerations

### Color Contrast
- Ensure text overlays meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards (4.5:1)
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning
- Provide alternative ways to communicate information

### Alternative Text
All illustrations should have descriptive alt text:

**Good Examples**:
- "Professional woman working on laptop in home office"
- "Celebration illustration showing successful job application"
- "Empty state illustration of magnifying glass searching"

**Bad Examples**:
- "Image" (too vague)
- "Illustration123.svg" (filename, not description)
- "" (empty, non-decorative images need descriptions)

### Reduced Motion
```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  .illustration--animated {
    animation: none;
    /* Show final state immediately */
  }
}
```

## Do's and Don'ts

### ✓ DO

1. **Keep it Simple**
   - Focus on one clear concept per illustration
   - Remove unnecessary details
   - Use clean, recognizable shapes

2. **Stay on Brand**
   - Use approved color palette
   - Follow gradient guidelines
   - Maintain consistent style across all illustrations

3. **Be Inclusive**
   - Represent diverse users
   - Avoid stereotypes
   - Show varied scenarios and settings

4. **Consider Context**
   - Match illustration complexity to usage
   - Ensure legibility at actual size
   - Test on intended backgrounds

5. **Optimize Files**
   - Compress for web
   - Use appropriate formats
   - Organize layers clearly

### ✗ DON'T

1. **Don't Over-complicate**
   - Avoid excessive detail
   - Don't use too many colors
   - Keep gradients subtle

2. **Don't Mix Styles**
   - No mixing flat with realistic
   - Maintain consistent character style
   - Keep scene aesthetics uniform

3. **Don't Use**
   - Stock photo elements
   - Clipart
   - Inconsistent art styles
   - Copyrighted characters/brands

4. **Don't Rely on Text**
   - Illustrations should communicate visually
   - Text should support, not carry meaning
   - Avoid illustrations that require explanation

5. **Don't Create**
   - Negative, discouraging imagery
   - Culturally insensitive content
   - Dated or trendy elements that age quickly
